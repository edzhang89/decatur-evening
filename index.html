The Deer and the Dove

155 Sycamore St, on the square · Directions · deerdove.com
James Beard: Best Chef Southeast '23Michelin GuideWhole-animal, wood-fired

The read

Terry Koval's farm-obsessed, hearth-driven flagship — the consensus pick for the best kitchen in Decatur. In-house butchery, house salumi, and a menu that name-checks its farms because the sourcing genuinely is the point. The Infatuation calls it the place in Atlanta for wild game; Michelin praises the rustic wood-fire cooking. The room is warm and unfussy — this is fine-dining ingredients with neighborhood-restaurant manners. It's game-heavy by design: rabbit, squab, venison, pheasant. Lean in.

How to order for four

Open with (share everything):

  • Salted yeast rolls — non-negotiable, order at hello$8
  • Burrata + GA peaches — peak-July Georgia on a plate$19
  • Chicken liver paté, peach jam — a signature; reviewers' constant$18
  • Beef tartare — ribeye tartare w/ gribiche + focaccia$29

Mains — the game plan:

  • Woodfired Half Rabbit — the signature. Legs + loin, herb risotto, Benton's ham. If the table shares one showpiece, it's this$57
  • Dry-Aged Duck — smoked breast + paté en croûte + foie; the dish critics write home about$59
  • Bucatini, chanterelles + sungolds — the "safe" order that isn't; great for Lisa if game's not her thing$32
  • Whole Trout — Georgia trout, green garlic butter; the light option$42
Feeling festive? The 40-day dry-aged Côte de Boeuf ($195) feeds the table with bone marrow and duck-fat potatoes — but it crowds out the game dishes that make this place this place. I'd skip it tonight.

Wine by the glass — value calls

I ran the list against retail. Standard markup here is ~3× retail per bottle; anything near 2× is a gift. Dots = value.

Dessert & digestifs at the table

Desserts: Crème caramel with sorghum ($10) is the pastry chef's quiet flex; chocolate fudge cake w/ cornflake ice cream ($14) for the table's chocolate vote. French press for two is $7.75 — spike it with their house coffee liqueur.

Amari — value math on the pour:

  • Cynar or Amaro CioCiaro — cheapest pours, both excellent digestifs. CioCiaro is the insider's Averna (which is $16 here)$11
  • Eda Rhyne Amaro Oscura or Nocino — small Asheville distillery, hard to find at retail. This is what "worth ordering out" means$14
  • Murrell's Row Barrel-Rested Eno — made in Atlanta, barrel-aged, can't buy it easily. The local-flex pour$18
  • PX Sherry + the crème caramel — the best $24 dessert-pairing move on both menus$14
  • Skip Braulio ($16) & Fernet ($12) — they're on your own bar shelf at home. Order what you can't pour yourself

Option A · Nightcap, Island-Style

🍹 S.O.S. Tiki Bar

340 W Ponce Court / Church St side · 4-min walk

Decatur's basement-vibe tiki den — blackout-dark, bamboo, shelves of collectible mugs (they'll sell you one), and a rum list far more serious than the décor lets on. Reviews consistently praise hand-built, properly boozy tiki drinks; big comfy booths make it easy for four to keep talking. The move post-dinner: Jungle Bird for you (rum + Campari — the amaro-lover's tiki drink, basically a digestif in a mug), Mai Tai as the benchmark, Zombie only if Charlie's committed to the bit.

Pick this if: the table wants one more real drink with maximum fun and zero pretense. Gets loud and full late on weekends — the earlier you land, the better the booth.

Option B · Dessert

🍦 Butter & Cream

105 E Ponce de Leon Ave · 2-min walk

Decatur's beloved small-batch ice cream shop — everything churned in-house. The move: brown butter brittle or a seasonal fruit flavor, in a fresh waffle cone, eaten walking the square.

Pick this if: dinner ran heavy on wine and you want air, a stroll, and a 20-minute nightcap-lite. Note: closes 10 PM most nights — it's the early-exit option.

Option C · One More Proper Drink

🥂 Kimball House

303 E Howard Ave · 6-min walk (old train depot)

One of the South's great cocktail bars — a restored 1891 depot with an absinthe program, deep classics, and a late oyster happy hour some nights. Reviewers rank the after-dinner cocktails among Atlanta's best; if the four of you want one impeccable drink in a gorgeous room, it's not close.

Pick this if: the conversation's too good to stop. Backup on the square: Brick Store Pub's upstairs Belgian bar — candlelit, quiet, 100+ bottle list, zero TVs.

Option D · Live Music — Tonight's Show

🎸 Eddie's Attic: Song Cycles

515-B N McDonough St · 3-min walk · Doors 8:45 · Show 9:00 PM

The legendary listening room (John Mayer and Sugarland played early sets here) hosts Song Cycles — an Atlanta songwriter showcase where five writers each play their own catalogue plus one brand-new song written to a shared theme, debuted live. Intimate room, respectful listening crowd — perfect timing off a 7:00 dinner.
